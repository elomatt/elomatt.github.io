Come for a Pint (of Science)!

4/4/2017

0 Comments

 
Pint of Science is back in Southampton for another year, with all your favourite sciencey topics: from planet Earth and the human body, to the way we understand atoms and galaxies!

Events will be running at bars, pubs and other venues across Southampton city centre and beyond. Exploring the extremes of physical science, Atoms to Galaxies is being graciously hosted again by Ebb & Flow on the 15th, 16th and 17th of May- a cosy, friendly venue with a great selection of soft and alcoholic drinks and food.

Tickets for each night are £4, and we only have a limited number left! They are available online at the following pages
  • 15th May: Bright Collisions and Good Vibrations
  • 16th May: Architecture with Atoms
  • 17th May: Black Holes and Revelations
Hope to see you there!

STEM for Britain Success!

29/3/2017

0 Comments

 
Back in the misty days of November, shortly after completing corrections to my thesis, I submitted an application to the "STEM for Britain" competition, run by the Parliamentary and Scientific Committee. I was privileged enough to be shortlisted for the competition and prepared a poster to present at the event at Portcullis House on 13th March this year.

On a lovely evening in the heart of Westminster, with many distinguished guests and a number of my fellow scientific colleagues who had brought fascinating pieces of work from across the physical sciences. There was much mingling and discussion and a very pleasant exchange of ideas, until the judges reached their decision, and I was lucky enough to be honoured with a silver award in physics and engineering!

I would like to thank the Committee, the judges, the MPs in attendance and all involved for putting on such a fantastic event.

New year, new opportunities

2/2/2016

0 Comments

 
Working hard on the PhD has left me with scant free time to pursue science communication opportunities in the first month of 2016, but that doesn't mean they're not there! The Science Room continues to run full steam ahead, with a great roster of events coming up at the Art House.

Looking further ahead, on 12th March, is the Southampton Science & Engineering festival. Together with some of the Outreach team at the University of Southampton and a few friends of my own we're looking to cook up something quite special for it.

Towards the end of March, I'll be participating in Southampton's Big Bang Fair, providing entertaining, educational and (hopefully) edifying information on career prospects in academic research, and what to expect from a STEM degree course.

Finally, on 23rd to 25th May you'll be able to find me <strike>freaking out as a little ball of stress</strike> really deeply enjoying being a part of the team helping to organise Pint of Science 2016.

There really is a lot to look forward to this spring!

Award-winning Engagement

19/11/2015

0 Comments

 
Receiving the award on behalf of #sciroom: Matthew Aldous and Jamal Kinsella
As a member of the Science Room team I was thrilled that our contribution to the University of Southampton Bringing Research to Life Roadshow 2015 was recognised at the annual awards ceremony yesterday evening. The award we received was for Innovation & Development as part of the Roadshow collaboration, reflecting the work Andrei Dragomir and I did in bringing a slice of the Science Room to the University's own Science Tent at Bestival!

Supporting us from back home was a strong team of researchers including Jamal (pictured above), Karolina, Zoe and many more. We hope to bring the Science Room back to the Roadshow time and time again, and look forward to more collaborations with other research institutions in the near future!

Introductions

14/11/2015

0 Comments

 
Hello and welcome to my community engagement blog.

Here you'll find the occasional update as to what I'm up to with the various organisations I work with. This year so far I've contributed as a founding member to The Science Room, an active participant in the Bringing Research to Life Roadshow at Bestival, and a member of the STEMNET ambassador programme.

One of the goals of the Science Room project is to be a multidisciplinary endeavour that marries artistic work with scientific concepts. The header of this page is a photo that was taken at the East Street Arts Festival, where we were invited to run a stall demonstrating colourful and exciting science demonstrations. This sort of work is what I find the most exciting part of science communication, and what I hope I have the privilege of continuing to do for a long time to come.

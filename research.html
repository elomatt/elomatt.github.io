﻿UK Quantum Technologies Showcase

20/11/2016

0 Comments

 
Early this month I had the privilege of participating in a showcase of work being done throughout the UK's National Quantum Technologies Hubs. A conference held in London gave me a wonderful opportunity to show off some of the stuff that we've been developing in Southampton, and to collaborate with exhibitors from other parts of the programme.

Alongside colleagues from Nottingham, I was pleased to present a miniature spectroscopy system using one of our cells as a frequency reference for locking a diode laser to the Rubidium cooling transition, all driven by a RedPitaya control board and, power supplies and all, ably lugged across London in a pair of flight cases by yours truly.
The day was a great opportunity to discuss my work with several peers and industry members with an interest in the field.

Introductions

14/11/2015

0 Comments

 
Hello and welcome to my research blog.

This page is where I'll be posting events and research outcomes that result from my work in the Integrated Atom Chips (IACs) research group at the University of Southampton. The IAC microMOT project is an innovative project which aims to gradually miniaturise all the essential components of a typical cold atom experiment, starting with the vacuum chamber and eventually including all the cooling and imaging optics and control electronics. The header for this page shows one of the most important components of this device: the grating MOT reflector. My research currently consists of integrating this into a machined silicon, centimetre-scale ultra-high vacuum (UHV) chamber using eutectic bonding,  and sealing said chamber with an optical window using anodic bonding.

In order to do this, I've spent the last three years designing and building a UHV aligner wafer bonder capable of producing these devices. Right now we're right in the middle of refitting the bonder for the third major revision, which will include the introduction of a 5-axis tilt stage to perfect the chip sealing process.

Watch this space for more mildly interesting information about the work happening in the basement of Building 46 on Highfield Campus!
A proposed design for the first generation of Integrated Atom Chip. From right to left, the layers are: flat optical input coupler, PCB carrying quadrupole field coil, sealing glass, silicon frame, grating MOT reflector layer and control PCB.

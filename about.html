About MAtt Aldous

Publication list available here
Born in Chelmsford, Essex in 1990, I spent many years living in the eastern English countryside before moving to Dorset in 1998. I completed my GCSEs and A-levels at Bournemouth School, as well as grade 8 violin (ABRSM). During this time I was also an active member of the Hampshire County Youth Orchestra, enjoying the opportunity to perform some amazing works in many exciting venues.

I then travelled to Exeter, commencing my studies to earn an MPhys Physics with European Study. For two years I followed lectures and carried out lab work by day, while performing in Exeter University Symphony Orchestra and two campus bands by night. In my spare time I also ran an award-winning alternative music show on Xpression FM ("Best Presenter" X-Media, 2012) and held a credit for Executive producer on Exeter's winning entry to the National Student Radio Awards for Best Student Radio Chart Show (2012). In my third year of undergraduate study, I moved to the beautiful city of Rennes in northwest France where I lived and studied for 8 months.

Upon my return to the UK I completed my MPhys project, carried out a three-month placement in a plasmonics research lab at Exeter,  graduated with a first class degree, and moved to Southampton. With the supervision of Dr. Matt Himsworth's Integrated Atom Chips (IAC) group I pursued a PhD in quantum technologies, completed in 2016.

After a brief period working as a post-doc with Matt I moved to the University of Birmingham to work in the strontium group with Dr. Yeshpal Singh. Immediately upon joining the project I began to lead a sub-team based at the National Physical Laboratory developing compact laser control and stabilisation systems for a miniature optical lattice clock project, due to complete in 2019.

I am also an active member of the Southampton and London science outreach communities, as a founding member of the Science Room project and a contributor to the Bringing Research to Life Roadshow. I also recently appeared as a science busker at a Bach, The Universe and Everything event with the Orchestra of the Age of Enlightenment and the Institute of Physics. To continue my musical career, I play in local folk bands Monkey See Monkey Do and The Flying Dishrags as a percussionist, vocalist and fiddler.

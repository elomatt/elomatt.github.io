A Fantastic Festival Season

31/8/2017

0 Comments

 
What a wonderful summer. In contrast to last year, when I was mostly cooped up with thesisitis, I have been able to get out and see some festivals and play some gigs. Sometimes even in the sunshine!
Starting with Cambridge Folk Festival in late July, where I had a fantastic time with Threepenny Bit who kicked off the weekend with an outstanding ceilidh set. They set a precedent for an energetic and entertaining few days, including performances by such luminaries as Mawkin, Urban Folk Theory (who are flipping awesome, by the way) and Frank Turner. Belshazzar's Feast even managed to get a "random member of the audience" to play percussion for part of their set!
My second ever Sidmouth festival was also great fun, although punctuated on the first Saturday by driving to Reading for a gig (which was certainly worth it as the ever-entertaining Derek Shaw kept me laughing throughout). Helping out Clausentum Morris as a "spare" musician, meant I had lots of dance spots to play for, but also plenty of spare time for sessions, ceilidh dancing and even a fiddle workshop with the unique Sam Sweeney! Highlights of the week were definitely the LNE performances by Threepenny Bit (about blooming time) and by Stumpy Oak, who are a great bunch to play with and to dance to. It was a week full of friendship, tunes, beer and a whole lot of dancing and I'm very much looking forward to next year.

The Flying Dishrags & The Talking Heads

20/11/2016

0 Comments

 
The last few months have been something of a wild ride, but in musical terms I've just been keeping up with the occasional Folksoc ceilidh while playing a few private gigs with Monkey See Monkey Do. Now that things are starting to settle a little though, plans for the future are coming together.

Monkey See are putting in our first public appearance for some time at the end of this week, as we have been booked for the Maple Leaf Lounge Session at the Talking Heads. This Friday at 8pm we'll be performing along with two other acts, playing our own brand of dynamic, folkish entertainment. I do hope you'll join us.

With an eye to the future, some more big changes are afoot! Along with some other reprobates who can often be seen gathering in some of the louder corners of Southampton pubs, I've started a new ceilidh band. Keep an eye out for The Flying Dishrags online, and do give us a like and say hello on Facebook. We are available for weddings and other private events, as well as planning to put on a launch ceilidh some time in the new year.

Lots of Ceilidhs & Gigs in the Winter Season!

13/12/2015

0 Comments

 
Things have been very busy in the last couple of weeks! Following our annual contribution to the Krakensoc Steampunk Ceilidh two weeks ago, last weekend FolkSoc was asked to appear at both the National Oceanographic Centre Society Christmas party on 4th December, and at the Friends of St James' Park Music and Christmas Tree Festival on the 5th. Both events were really good fun, and it was great to be a part of the celebration. We've since been informed that the Christmas Tree Festival raised £3,000 for good causes in and around the parish.

​During the week we had our usual FolkSoc fun, with a rip-roaring session at the Stile on Monday night, and a fun Christmas social on Wednesday, rounding off the university term with a trip to Winchester for an always-fabulous Discovery Centre ceilidh with the lovely Threepenny Bit who invited Lewis, Gemma and Rob, and I to help warm up the winter crowds. It was good hot and sweaty fun, and we managed to stay mostly upright all evening!

Monkey See Monkey Do also managed to squeeze in a band practice on Thursday night, where we nailed down a couple of arrangements that we're hoping to get recorded in the near future. The very near future: watch this space!

Folksoc & Mountaineering Ceilidh﻿

14/11/2015

0 Comments

 
Last Saturday was the brilliant Mountaineering Society ceilidh, run as a collaboration with the Folk Society. We had dancers filling the entire Bridge and a large contingent of musicians who produced a massive sound. Several members of Folksoc (myself included) acted as guest callers, helping the night to go (mostly) smoothly! As far as we know, a good time was had by all!
